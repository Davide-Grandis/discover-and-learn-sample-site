Artificial Intelligence

From neural networks to large language models — machines that learn and reason.

What Is AI?

Artificial Intelligence is the field of computer science dedicated to creating systems that can perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence — recognising speech, understanding language, making decisions, and generating creative content.

While the dream of thinking machines dates back centuries, modern AI began in the 1950s when Alan Turing posed his famous question: "Can machines think?"

Types of AI

  • Narrow AI (ANI) — designed for a specific task, like image recognition or language translation. This is all AI that exists today.
  • General AI (AGI) — a hypothetical system with human-level reasoning across all domains. Still a research goal.
  • Superintelligent AI (ASI) — surpasses human intelligence in every field. Purely theoretical at this point.

How Machine Learning Works

Most modern AI is powered by machine learning — algorithms that improve through experience rather than explicit programming. The three main approaches are:

  1. Supervised Learning — the model trains on labelled data (e.g., photos tagged "cat" or "dog")
  2. Unsupervised Learning — the model finds hidden patterns in unlabelled data
  3. Reinforcement Learning — the model learns through trial and error, receiving rewards for good decisions

Deep learning, a subset of machine learning, uses artificial neural networks with many layers to learn increasingly abstract representations of data.

"AI is the new electricity. Just as electricity transformed almost everything 100 years ago, today I actually have a hard time thinking of an industry that I don't think AI will transform." — Andrew Ng

AI in Everyday Life

You probably interact with AI dozens of times a day without realising it:

  • Voice assistants — Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant
  • Recommendations — Netflix, Spotify, and YouTube suggestions
  • Navigation — real-time traffic prediction in Google Maps
  • Photography — computational photography on your smartphone
  • Email — spam filters and smart replies
  • Healthcare — AI-assisted diagnosis from medical imaging
