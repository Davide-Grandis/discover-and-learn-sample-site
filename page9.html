Renewable Energy

Solar, wind, hydro, and beyond — the future of sustainable power.

Why Renewables Matter

Fossil fuels — coal, oil, and natural gas — have powered human progress for over two centuries, but at a steep environmental cost. Burning them releases greenhouse gases that drive climate change, air pollution, and ocean acidification.

Renewable energy sources offer a path to powering civilisation without these destructive side effects. And thanks to dramatic cost reductions, they are now economically competitive with fossil fuels in most markets.

The Numbers

Renewable energy adoption is accelerating worldwide:

  • Solar costs have dropped by over 90% since 2010
  • Wind power is now the cheapest source of new electricity in many countries
  • Global capacity exceeded 3,800 GW in 2023, with solar leading new installations
  • Battery storage costs have fallen 90% in a decade, solving the intermittency problem
"The Stone Age didn't end because we ran out of stones. The fossil fuel age will end because we found something better." — Sheikh Yamani

What You Can Do

Individual actions add up. Consider:

  1. Switching to a green energy tariff from your utility provider
  2. Installing solar panels if you own your home
  3. Reducing energy consumption with efficient appliances and insulation
  4. Supporting policies that promote clean energy investment
