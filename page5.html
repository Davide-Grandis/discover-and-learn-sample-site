Music Through the Ages

From ancient drums to modern electronic beats — a history of human rhythm.

The Universal Language

Music is one of the few things found in every known human culture, past and present. The oldest known musical instrument — a bone flute found in a cave in Slovenia — dates back over 60,000 years.

Whether used in religious ceremony, storytelling, or celebration, music has always been a fundamental part of what it means to be human.

Major Musical Eras

EraPeriodKey Features
Medieval500–1400Gregorian chant, monophonic texture, religious focus
Renaissance1400–1600Polyphony, secular themes, early printed music
Baroque1600–1750Ornamentation, counterpoint, Bach & Vivaldi
Classical1750–1820Clarity, balance, Mozart & Beethoven
Romantic1820–1900Emotional expression, large orchestras, Chopin & Wagner
Modern1900–presentJazz, rock, electronic, hip-hop, genre fusion

Genres That Shaped the 20th Century

  • Jazz — born in New Orleans, blending African rhythms with European harmony
  • Blues — the foundation of rock and roll, rooted in African-American experience
  • Rock & Roll — electrified the world in the 1950s with Chuck Berry and Elvis
  • Hip-Hop — emerged from the Bronx in the 1970s, now a global cultural force
  • Electronic — synthesisers and drum machines created entirely new sonic worlds
