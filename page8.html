Arduino & Microcontrollers

Tiny computers that bring ideas to life — sensors, motors, LEDs, and endless possibilities.

What Is a Microcontroller?

A microcontroller is a small, self-contained computer on a single chip. It has a processor, memory, and input/output pins — everything needed to read sensors, control motors, blink LEDs, and communicate with other devices.

Unlike a full computer, microcontrollers are designed for dedicated tasks: measuring temperature, controlling a robot arm, or automating your home. They're cheap, low-power, and everywhere — from your washing machine to your car.

The Arduino Ecosystem

Arduino revolutionised electronics by making microcontrollers accessible to everyone. Founded in 2005 in Ivrea, Italy, the project combined affordable open-source hardware with a simple IDE and a C/C++ based language.

Key components of the Arduino ecosystem:

  • Arduino IDE — a free, cross-platform editor for writing and uploading code (called "sketches")
  • Shields — stackable add-on boards for motor control, Ethernet, GPS, displays, and more
  • Libraries — thousands of community-contributed libraries for sensors, protocols, and peripherals
  • Community — one of the largest maker communities in the world, with forums, tutorials, and shared projects

Your First Sketch: Blink an LED

The classic "Hello, World!" of microcontrollers — blinking the built-in LED on pin 13:

void setup() {
  pinMode(13, OUTPUT);
}

void loop() {
  digitalWrite(13, HIGH);
  delay(1000);
  digitalWrite(13, LOW);
  delay(1000);
}

Upload this sketch to your Arduino, and the onboard LED will blink on and off every second. From here, the possibilities are endless.

Project Ideas

  1. Weather Station — use a DHT22 sensor to log temperature and humidity to an LCD display
  2. Line-Following Robot — infrared sensors guide a small car along a black line
  3. Smart Plant Watering — soil moisture sensor triggers a pump when your plants are thirsty
  4. MIDI Controller — potentiometers and buttons that send MIDI messages to music software
  5. Home Automation — ESP32 + relays to control lights and appliances over Wi-Fi
