World Cuisines

A culinary tour of iconic dishes and cooking traditions from around the globe.

Food as Culture

Every cuisine tells a story — of geography, climate, trade routes, and the people who shaped it. What we eat and how we prepare it reveals deep truths about our history and identity.

From the spice markets of Marrakech to the street stalls of Bangkok, food connects us all while celebrating our differences.

The Five Mother Sauces

French chef Auguste Escoffier codified five foundational sauces that underpin Western cooking:

  1. Béchamel — butter, flour, and milk
  2. Velouté — light stock thickened with a roux
  3. Espagnole — brown stock, tomato paste, and mirepoix
  4. Hollandaise — egg yolk, butter, and lemon
  5. Tomato — tomatoes, garlic, and herbs
