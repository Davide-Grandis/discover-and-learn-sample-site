Ocean Wonders

Dive into the deep blue — coral reefs, mysterious trenches, and marine life.

The Blue Planet

Oceans cover more than 70% of Earth's surface and contain about 97% of all water on the planet. They regulate our climate, produce over half our oxygen, and harbour an astonishing diversity of life.

Yet we have explored less than 20% of the ocean floor — meaning there is more unmapped territory beneath the waves than on the surface of Mars.

Coral Reefs: Rainforests of the Sea

Coral reefs occupy less than 1% of the ocean floor but support roughly 25% of all marine species. The Great Barrier Reef alone stretches over 2,300 kilometres and is visible from space.

  • Hard corals build the reef structure by secreting calcium carbonate
  • Soft corals sway like underwater trees, providing shelter for small fish
  • Symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae) live within coral tissue, giving reefs their vivid colours
"The sea, once it casts its spell, holds one in its net of wonder forever." — Jacques Cousteau

Deep-Sea Creatures

In the abyssal zone, where sunlight never reaches, life has evolved bizarre adaptations:

  • Anglerfish — lure prey with a bioluminescent dangling light
  • Giant Squid — can grow up to 13 metres, with the largest eyes in the animal kingdom
  • Vampire Squid — not actually a squid; it drifts through oxygen-minimum zones
  • Dumbo Octopus — flaps ear-like fins to "fly" through the deep water
